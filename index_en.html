About

Hi, my name is Pinja Turunen. Nice to make your acquaintance. After the previous I usually like to start telling a little bit about myself, my academic background and workhistory. However, for now I would like to take the time to get to the most important details which truly make me, well, me....

I get excited about the discussions that truly make you reshape yourself and your views. Not just in my work environment but in life itself. I don't like to spend my time repeating something that most people could figure out in minutes. We live in a global network after all and details about a persons work history or academic achievements are fastly available.

So let's get down to business. What are your deepest passions, fears and goals? What has shaped you as a person? These are the discussions in which I feel the most invested in. I tend to be as open as I can, I think that honesty and openess between all parties is essential in every interaction that we have on a day to day basis.

If you haven't guessed by now, I am deeply invested in people and that has everything to do with my passion in psychology. My dream has been to be a psychologist for a long time but before anything else all I want to do is help people like I have been helped throughout my life.

I am also very passionate about making art and being creative in my everyday life. I enjoy the small things that make life worth living for. For example I love being surrounded by nature and seeing all of the small wonders that usually get looked past on.

After all this you must be wondering, what are my deepest fears since I have already told you my deepest passions. To put it simply, I fear to live an unfulfilling life. By this I mean a life in which I can't be true to myself and others. I want to be as understanding as possible, not just to everyone else but to myself as well. I have and continue to try to live by the words of the late painter Bob Ross:

"There are no mistakes, just happy little accidents."

I like so many more have faced challenges that could have been caused by various reasons, sometimes our greatest challenge is even us ourselves. However I try to look past this and make the best out of the situation with the tools I have. I look forward to challenging myself and others and I am deeply appreciative for everyone who is open to that as well.

Thank you for getting to know me, you're welcome to return the favour simply by saying hi and we'll see how it goes from there.
